Common standard platform rollout for Asia and Europe

IT Coordinator, System Manager

Background

The necessity of providing customers and co-workers with a standardized IT Infrastructure globally became the very goal for being able to maintain hardware and business applications efficiently. Since 1997 a standard desktop platform was established in certain countries. With this particular rollout the, standard platform was to be distributed worldwide. With the new version, containing Windows NT 4.0 on servers and Windows 98 on desktop clients, Asia was the first continent to receive a new and homogenous IT Infrastructure. Followed by Europe and US the standard platform concept still exists today at the customers organization.

Scope of transformation

Asia was the first continent to be rollout with the standard platform, called Common Client 2.0. Since that continent never had a standard setup on its IT Infrastructure it was a good candidate for implementation. New Servers, new clients both on hardware level and software level where established - both in stores, warehouses and offices. The project was initiated by the Swedish central IT office, containing installation teams from Sweden, Austria, France and Asia. The local IT in Asia was to be involved and trained by teamleads and main responsible for each installation site.

Scope of my deliverables

  • Teamlead of small installation teams of 3 people
  • Coordination with local management
  • SPOC for escalation of onsite problems to IT Management in Europe
  • Training/education of the concept and maintanance for local IT personel in Asia
  • Travelling to trading and retail offices to secure proper setup of hardware and software
  • Installation of Common Desktop Platform together with local IT personal
  • Extended User Support for troubleshooting post installation issues
  • Handover to local IT
  • System Exchange and technical guidance from Windows 3.51 to Windows NT 4.0
