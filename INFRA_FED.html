Infrastructure Upgrade - Federal Tax & Customs Offices

Team lead, Infrastructure Technician

Background

1994 was an important year for Austrians Federal Tax Offices and Customs offices. The IT department of Austrian Federal Computing Centre decided to upgrade their IT Infrastructure countrywide. At that time mainly IBM equipment was used in all areas of IT. The goal to enhance their TokenRing network as part of the general growth of computers, replacing old machines with new ones and updating the software Banyan Vines 2.0 to the next available version was to be accomplished within one year. The scope included 10 tax offices and 9 customs offices with a total of 500 users countrywide.

Scope of transformation

The project itself was lead by the project manager of both the Austrian Federal Computing Centre and IBM. Concept decisions were made by stakeholders within the Federal Computing Centre. Implementation was outsourced to small installation teams with each 2 people from IBM. Travelling onsite to every location was required to perform actual work. Every teamlead held responsible for his/her site to be upgraded according to the requirements and standards of involved stakeholders.

Scope of my deliverables

  • Implementation team responsible - teamlead
  • Coordination of rollout countrywide
  • Time and rollout coordination with local IT managers/personel
  • Network adaptions/enhancements on TokenRing network locally
  • Troubleshooting of malfunctions
  • Coordination of hardware and software exchange
  • Replacement of old client and server hardware, new software installation of the same
  • System upgrade of Banyan Vines 2.0 to the next available version
