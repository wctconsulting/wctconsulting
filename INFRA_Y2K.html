Y2K problem Millenium Bug

System Manger

Background

The Y2K problem, also called Millenium Bug was related to the formatting and storage of calendar data for dates beginning in the year 2000. Problems were anticipated, because many computer programs represented four-digit years with only the final two digits. This would mean that the year 2000 would be indistinguishable from 1900. As a result programs could cause various errors, such as incorrect display of dates and inaccurate ordering of automated dated records or real-time events.

Scope of transformation

An IT Rollover plan was established to describe activities necessary to perform to minimize disturbances and malfunctions due to possible failures from year 1999 to 2000 and prove y2k readiness in all business units globally. In focus are all areas where no tests have been performed on an earlier stage, e. g. unique combinations of IT infrastructure in each location. Minor disturbances were accepted, but in general the security of customers, visitors, employees and IKEA property was to guarantee. In detail it was required to secure that stores could sell and be paid, warehouses could receive and deliver their goods and offices could perform their daily business critical work. An IT Rollover Plan was established to cover all necessary steps and activities.

Scope of my deliverables
